Singer Michael Jackson

The man who has the most interesting life story

Here is the lifetime line of MJ:

  • 1958 - Born in Gary, Indiana, US
  • 1958–1975 - Sing in the band Jackson 5 with his brothers (Jackie, Tito, Jermaine, Marlon, and Randy).
  • 1975–1981 - Sing in the band with new name Jacksons.
  • 1982–1983 - Starting solo carrier, in 1982 first album "Thriller" (best selling album in 1983), soundtrack and audiobook for the film E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial.
  • 1984–1985- Pepsi commercials in 1984, and in 1985 he published the song "We are the world" to raise money and help the people in Africa.
  • 1986–1987 - That was the period when tabloids started to speculate about the surgeries that Mj has done to change his skin color and face.
  • 1987–1990 - Jackson's first album in five years, Bad (1987), In 1988, Jackson released his autobiography, Moonwalk.In March 1988, Jackson purchased 2,700 acres (11 km2) of land near Santa Ynez, California, to build a new home, Neverland Ranch, at a cost of $17 million.Jackson became known as the "King of Pop", a nickname that Jackson's publicists embraced. When Elizabeth Taylor presented him with the Soul Train Heritage Award in 1989, she called him "the true king of pop, rock and soul.
  • 1991–1993 - In March 1991, Jackson renewed his contract with Sony and he released his eighth album, Dangerous. Jackson founded the Heal the World Foundation in 1992. In January 1993, Jackson won three American Music Awards: Favorite Pop/Rock Album (Dangerous), Favorite Soul/R&B Single ("Remember the Time"), and was the first to win the International Artist Award of Excellence
  • 1993–1995 - First child sexual abuse accusations and first marriage. In August 1993, Jackson was accused of child sexual abuse. In late 1993, Jackson proposed to Lisa Marie Presley, the daughter of Elvis Presley. Jackson composed music for the Sega Genesis video game Sonic the Hedgehog 3 (1994).
  • 1995–1997 - In June 1995, Jackson released the double album HIStory: Past, Present and Future, Book I. The first single from HIStory was "Scream/Childhood". "Scream", a duet with Jackson's youngest sister Janet, protests the media's treatment of Jackson during the 1993 child abuse allegations against him. On July 1996, Jackson performed for Sultan Hassanal Bolkiah's fiftieth birthday at Jerudong Park Amphitheater, which was specifically built for that birthday concert. Jackson promoted HIStory with the HIStory World Tour, from September 7, 1996, to October 15, 1997. He performed 82 concerts in five continents, 35 countries and 58 cities to over 4.5 million fans, his most attended tour. It grossed $165 million. During the tour, in Sydney, Australia, Jackson married Debbie Rowe, a dermatology assistant, who was six months pregnant with his first child. Michael Joseph Jackson Jr. (commonly known as Prince) was born on February 13, 1997. His sister Paris-Michael Katherine Jackson was born on April 3, 1998.[233] Jackson and Rowe divorced in 2000, Rowe conceded custody of the children, with an $8 million settlement. In 2004, after the second child abuse allegations against Jackson, she returned to court to reclaim custody. The suit was settled in 2006. In 1997, Jackson released Blood on the Dance Floor: HIStory in the Mix.
  • 1997–2002 - From October 1997 to September 2001, Jackson worked on his tenth solo album. In September 2001, two 30th Anniversary concerts were held at Madison Square Garden to mark Jackson's 30th year as a solo artist. Jackson performed with his brothers for the first time since 1984. On January 9, 2002, Jackson won his 22nd American Music Award for Artist of the Century.[251][252] Later that year, an anonymous surrogate mother gave birth to his third child, Prince Michael Jackson II.
  • 2002–2005- Documentary, Number Ones, second child abuse allegations and acquittal. Beginning in May 2002, a documentary film crew led by Martin Bashir followed Jackson for several months. On December 18, 2003, Santa Barbara authorities charged Jackson with seven counts of child molestation and two counts of intoxicating a minor with alcoholic drinks.
  • 2005–2009 - Final years, financial problems, Thriller 25 and This Is It. In 2008, for the 25th anniversary of Thriller, Jackson and Sony released Thriller 25, with two remixes released as singles: "The Girl Is Mine 2008" and "Wanna Be Startin' Somethin' 2008". In March 2009, amid speculation about his finances and health, Jackson announced a series of comeback concerts, This Is It, at a press conference at the O2 Arena. The concerts were to run from July 13, 2009, to March 6, 2010.
  • 2009 - On June 25, 2009, less than three weeks before his concert residency was due to begin in London, with all concerts sold out, Jackson (age 50) died from cardiac arrest, caused by a propofol and benzodiazepine overdose.
